Break the state.

Pick one. Do it now. Don't think about it.

The urge lives in a particular mental state. You don't fight it — you move into a different state. Any of these work. The only rule is: pick one immediately and do it.

Call or text someone
Connection is what your nervous system is actually asking for. You don't need a reason to call.
Most powerful option ↓

Pick up your phone and call — not text if you can. A real voice works faster than anything else. You've already proven this: it's what stopped the binge-purge cycle before. That's not a coincidence.

If you can't call right now: Send a voice note, or text "can I call you?" The act of reaching out interrupts the isolated state the urge needs to grow in.

Go outside — even for two minutes
Leave the building. Change your physical environment completely.
Environment shift ↓

Put on shoes and walk out the front door. No destination needed. You just need to not be in the same physical space where the urge started.

Cold air, different sounds, the sky — all interrupt the sensory loop the urge runs on. Even standing on your doorstep for two minutes changes the state enough to let the wave begin to pass.

Move your body
Walk around, stretch, shake out your hands. Gets you out of your head and back into your body.
Physical state change ↓

You don't need to exercise. Walk from room to room. Stretch on the floor. Roll your shoulders. Shake your hands out. Any movement that brings you back into your physical body rather than your head.

Even better: Put music on and let yourself move to one song. You cannot be fully in an autopilot binge state and fully present in your body at the same time — they compete.

Put a specific playlist on
Not shuffle. A playlist you know and love. Music that means something to you.
Mood shift ↓

The urge runs on a particular internal mood. Music you love shifts that mood neurologically — not just distraction, but a genuine state change.

Have the playlist ready before you need it. One you associate with feeling good, or calm, or like yourself. Press play without thinking about it. The thinking is where the urge lives.

You don't have to feel like doing any of these. You just have to do one. The feeling follows the action — it never goes the other way around when you're in this state.

Who do I want to be?

Not a rule. Not a standard to fail. A direction. This is the person you are moving towards — and she is already partly you.

When I think about the person
I want to be —
She is kind to herself
She doesn't punish herself for being human. She responds to a hard evening the way she'd respond to a friend — with gentleness, not shame.
She is present in her evenings
Her evenings belong to her — not to the kitchen, not to the scroll, not to a pattern she didn't choose. She fills them with things that actually feel like living.
She reaches for people, not food
When she's lonely, stressed, or low — she calls someone. She's already shown this works. That version of her exists. She's been her before.
She is not defined by the hard nights
A hard night is one data point. It doesn't make her someone who can't do this. She knows the pattern, she has the tools, and she keeps going regardless.
She is free from the cycle
She has already been this person — for a whole relationship's worth of time, she was free. That wasn't luck. That was her. She can be her again.
